Barry Engagement Plan

Strategy for meeting with Barry Port, CEO of Ensign Group

April 2026 — Prepared by Andrew

Step 1

The Text Message

Short, warm, peer-to-peer. Don't sell. Create curiosity. Get in the room.

Hey Barry — hope you're doing well. Had a great meeting with Ryan and Tom a few weeks back. Got to show them the agentic platform I built after our lunch and walk through the architecture. Something clicked for me during that meeting that I think would be worth 30-60 minutes of your time this week. Not a pitch — just want to show you what I'm seeing in the market that I don't think anyone on your team is telling you. If you're open to it, I'll come to you. If not, no worries at all — our friendship matters way more than any business conversation.

Why This Works

  • References Ryan/Tom meeting — shows momentum, Barry likely already heard about it
  • "Something clicked" — curiosity hook without revealing what
  • "No one on your team is telling you" — CEO's biggest fear is blind spots
  • "Not a pitch" — disarms defensiveness
  • "I'll come to you" — zero friction
  • "Friendship matters more" — genuine, removes pressure that makes him avoid responding
  • No mention of the app, no links, no attachments — save the show for the meeting

Do Not Include

  • No video (save for follow-up if no response in 48 hours)
  • No links to the app or presentation
  • No mention of PACS/Hansen/Hunter (comes across as pressure)
  • No technical terms (Bedrock, VPC, agentic)
Step 2

The 60-Minute Meeting

You have one hour. Barry is smart, skeptical, and getting bad advice from people with financial incentives. Here's how to structure every minute.

Minutes 0–10

The Market Reality Not Your Product

Pull up stock charts: Workday down 38% to five-year low. $2T in SaaS market cap erased. Nadella quote: "SaaS applications collapse in the agentic era." Benioff cut 4,000 people because agents replaced them. Show Barry's Q4 quote on screen — then the Blockbuster analogy.

Minutes 10–20

The T-Ledger Analogy Anchor Concept

"Right now, Ensign is running a $4B company where every department head manually logs into PCC, Workday, SharePoint — that's the T-ledger. What I've built is the Excel macro layer. Agents do 99% of the work. One person per department reviews and approves." Show the DecisionCard concept — self-contained analyst briefing, under 10 seconds to act.

Minutes 20–40

Live Demo Show Don't Tell

Open the app. Walk through Command Center → Morning Standup → one clinical page → one financial page. Show a DecisionCard: "The agent already pulled the resident name, room number, medication, dosage, CMS citation, dollar impact. Your DON doesn't open PCC. She approves or escalates in 5 seconds." Show the 330-facility heatmap. Click into a facility. Show dark mode, tablet responsive — Apple quality.

Minutes 40–50

Address His Fears Critical

Hallucinations: Confidence scores. 95%+ = certain. Below 80% = human reviews. Nothing auto-executes.

Credentials: Agents never see raw passwords. Vault holds them. Proxy injects at request time. Agent can't leak what it doesn't have.

HIPAA: AWS Bedrock inside YOUR VPC. PHI never leaves your cloud. BAA-covered, SOC 2, HITRUST. Actually more secure than current SaaS vendors.

Minutes 50–60

The Ask Close

"I'm not asking you to commit to anything today. I'm asking for 30 days and four credential files — PCC, Workday, Azure AD, AWS. In 30 days, this goes from demo to live across all 330 facilities. If it doesn't deliver, you've lost nothing. If it does, you've just leapfrogged every competitor in the industry."

"Barry, the people advising you have a financial incentive to tell you their roadmap includes AI. But they can't cannibalize their own product. That's not how disruption works."

Step 3 — Backup Play

The 30-Second Video

Only send if Barry doesn't respond to the text within 48 hours. Talking head with Command Center on screen behind you. Casual — phone video is fine.

"Hey Barry — quick 30-second thing. After meeting with Ryan and Tom, I wanted to show you one screen from what I built.

[screen shows Command Center with DecisionCards]

This is what one person running an entire department looks like. Every card is a complete decision — the agent already pulled the data from PCC, Workday, all your systems. The human just approves or escalates. No logging into five different apps. No reports. No dashboards. Just decisions.

Would love 30 minutes to walk you through it. Talk soon."

Production Notes

  • Film with Command Center page on screen behind you (or screen share)
  • Keep it casual — phone video, don't over-produce
  • End with the ask — "30 minutes"
  • Don't send the video with the initial text — it's your follow-up move
