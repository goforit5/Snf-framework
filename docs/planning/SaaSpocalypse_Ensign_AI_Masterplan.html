Why The Market Crashed: 4 Reasons (Per Forrester)

1. AI Won't Need SaaS Vendors
SaaS companies will not be the provider of choice for AI agents. Enterprises will deploy AI agents directly, without needing traditional SaaS platforms as intermediaries.
2. Per-Seat Model Becomes Obsolete
AI agents handle work that humans used to do. Fewer seats needed = revenue decline. The per-seat SaaS model assumes more users, not fewer.
3. Vibe Coding Erodes Moats
With Claude Code at 80.9% SWE-bench, startups can replicate complex SaaS platforms in weeks. Software moats—built on 10+ years of engineering—can be recreated as an AI agent. Defensibility collapses.
4. SaaS Sprawl Is the Killer
Enterprises struggle with 300+ non-integrated SaaS apps. AI agents consolidate functions into single workflows. This creates pressure to consolidate, not proliferate, software spending.
The Budget Shift: "AI Isn't Eating the Product. It's Eating the Budget"

Why Investors Ignored It: Gross retention ≠ net-new customer acquisition. Investors are terrified of negative net-new logo growth, which is already appearing in the data.
SaaStr Perspective: Vibe Coding Isn't The Threat
Claim: "Vibe coding isn't killing Salesforce. 98% of enterprise software is scaling, maintaining, iterating. Not shipping a v1."

Why Investors Ignored It: Accurate, but irrelevant to pricing. If customers no longer NEED Salesforce—if an AI agent can replicate 80% of functionality—the threat isn't killing the company, it's shrinking the TAM.
Forrester's Bullish Case: SaaS Spending Will RISE
Projection: Global SaaS spending projected to rise from $318B (2025) → $512B (2028) → $576B (2029).

Why Investors Weren't Convinced: Total TAM growth ≠ individual company growth. TAM can expand while company market share contracts. This is what happened in the PC market (TAM exploded, but margins compressed).
Mindset AI: The Logical Contradiction
Point: "For the tech sector to collapse, two mutually exclusive things must be true: AI capex is deteriorating AND AI is so effective it's making tech worthless."

Why Investors Ignored It: This is a clever argument, but markets don't care about logic—they care about psychology. The psychology has shifted from growth to harvest. That's the real story.

What He's Celebrating: A team of "data services folks" who manually package data into dashboards.

Clinical AI is not the future. It's the present. Johns Hopkins, HCA Healthcare, Mayo Clinic are all deploying clinical AI now. Ensign is looking "into the future" when competitors are implementing today.

Section 5: The 10-Second Manager Review

How agentic AI transforms operational dashboards into decision-support systems that executives can actually use.

The Concept
Every morning, every department head receives a one-page AI-generated operational brief. It's designed to be read in 10 seconds. Everything else follows from that constraint.

What the Brief Contains

Color-Coded Status
Green: All metrics on track. No action needed.
Yellow: One or more metrics trending toward target miss. Attention needed.
Red: Critical issue. Immediate action required.

The brief shows only green/yellow/red items. Green items are omitted (don't waste executive time).
Overnight Events
Any significant events that occurred overnight:
  • Hospital discharge surge
  • Staff callouts affecting tomorrow
  • Census dips or gains
  • Quality metric anomalies
Today's Top 3 Priorities
AI has determined the three highest-impact things the manager should focus on today. Ranked by impact. Everything else is background work.
Predicted Issues (Next 24-48 Hours)
AI has looked at current trajectory and predicted what could go wrong in the next 24-48 hours. Preventative list. Manager can act proactively.
Recommended Actions
Specific, actionable recommendations for addressing yellow/red items. Not vague insights—concrete actions with predicted impact.
Context & Supporting Data
Quick facts to support decisions (vs. making the manager dig through dashboards):
  • Current occupancy & trend
  • Denial rate & biggest denial driver
  • Staff availability for today

Why This Transforms Operations

No time spent digging through dashboards, generating reports, or trying to figure out what matters. AI has done the analysis. Manager can act.

An AI agent can integrate data from 8+ systems (EHR, billing, scheduling, HR, quality) in seconds. No human can do this. The AI view is always more accurate.

Managers spend their time fighting fires (reacting). AI-generated briefs predict fires before they start. Manager can prevent problems instead of solving them.

Decisions Are Faster and Better Documented
Because the briefing is standardized, decisions are faster. And everything is logged for compliance/audit purposes.
Staff Focus on Operations, Not Administration
Instead of "data services folks" packaging dashboards, those staff can work on higher-value projects (improvement initiatives, new revenue streams, etc.).

The Chain Reaction

Better Decisions → Better Operations → Better Financial Results → Better Satisfaction → Better Retention

This is not speculation. This is a causal chain that's repeatable across 300 facilities. The operations that implement agentic AI will outperform those that don't. In 12-18 months, the gap will be undeniable.
