## SECTION 1: THE PR PLAYBOOK — RULES OF ENGAGEMENT

### The Golden Rule: Say Enough to Move the Stock, Not Enough to Help Competitors

Ensign's communications should signal **conviction and momentum** to investors while revealing **zero operational detail** that a competitor could copy. Think of this like Apple announcing "we're building something incredible" vs. showing the blueprints.

### What World-Class Companies Do When Announcing AI Initiatives

I studied press releases and earnings calls from the last 90 days. Here's how the best companies frame it:

**SEI Investments + IBM (released March 9, 2026 — today):**
- Announced "enterprise transformation through agentic AI"
- Used the phrase: *"investing in how we operate is just as critical as investing in what we deliver"*
- Named the partner (IBM) but revealed ZERO about specific workflows being automated
- Framed as "sustainable growth" not "cost cutting"

**Humana + Google Cloud (February 3, 2026):**
- Announced "Agent Assist" — a specific product name that sounds impressive but reveals nothing about the underlying tech
- Framed it as *"redefining how technology can strengthen human connection in healthcare"*
- Lead with member experience, not cost savings
- Emphasized "responsible, transparent and human-centered use of artificial intelligence"

**HCA Healthcare + Google Cloud + GE HealthCare:**
- Announced specific clinical use case (perinatal care) — showed domain expertise
- Named it "CareIntellect" — branded the AI, made it proprietary-sounding
- Emphasized "real-time, actionable insights directly at the point of care"
- Led with patient outcomes, not technology

### The Pattern

Every sophisticated company follows the same formula:

1. **Lead with mission, not technology** — "We're improving patient care" not "We're deploying GPT-4"
2. **Signal investment and conviction** — make it clear this is strategic, not experimental
3. **Brand the initiative** — give it a name that sounds proprietary (even if the underlying tech is standard)
4. **Emphasize human-centered design** — "AI supports our clinicians" not "AI replaces people"
5. **Reference compliance** — mention HIPAA, data security, responsible AI
6. **Be vague about specifics** — never reveal which workflows, which tools, which vendors, or which facilities
7. **Tie to existing strengths** — connect AI to what investors already love about the company

## SECTION 2: WHAT ENSIGN SHOULD SAY

### The Core Narrative (Investor-Facing)

> *Ensign has made a significant strategic commitment to AI-powered care delivery and operational excellence. We are building proprietary AI capabilities — leveraging our unmatched clinical dataset across 300+ facilities — that will amplify the clinical quality and operational discipline that our local leaders already deliver every day. This initiative is designed to accelerate our organic growth, enhance our acquisition transition process, and deepen our competitive advantages in the markets we serve. We are deploying these capabilities within a rigorous HIPAA-compliant architecture with human-in-the-loop safeguards, ensuring that AI supports and augments our caregivers rather than replacing the human connection that defines Ensign.*

### Suggested Initiative Name

Do NOT call it "AI" in external communications. Name it something proprietary that sounds like Ensign's own capability:

| Option | Why It Works |
|--------|-------------|
| **Ensign Intelligence Platform** | Sounds proprietary. Signals capability without revealing anything |
| **CareEngine** | Mirrors HCA's "CareIntellect" — clinical, branded, defensible |
| **Ensign Insight** | Clean. Suggests data-driven decision-making without the AI buzzword |
| **The Mission Accelerator** | Ties to Ensign's language about "mission to dignify post-acute care" |

My recommendation: **"Ensign Insight"** — it's simple, it sounds like a product, it doesn't scream "we bought ChatGPT."

### Anticipated Analyst Questions & Prepared Responses

**Q: "Can you give us more detail on the AI investment? What specific tools or platforms are you using?"**

**BARRY PORT:**
*"We're being intentionally measured in what we share publicly on this, and I think you can understand why. What I will say is that we've built this on enterprise-grade, HIPAA-compliant infrastructure using the leading cloud AI platforms. We chose not to partner with a single large technology vendor for the same reason we don't run our operations from a central office — we want the flexibility to choose the best tools for each use case and the ability to move quickly. Our team has deep expertise in both the technology and the skilled nursing business, which is a combination that's frankly very hard to find."*

---

**Q: "How should we think about the financial impact? Is this a headwind to margins in 2026?"**

**SUZANNE SNAPPER (CFO):**
*"The investment is meaningful but disciplined — consistent with how we approach everything at Ensign. We expect net-positive ROI within the first year of deployment. The cost structure is significantly lower than a traditional enterprise software implementation because of how we've architected it. We're not buying a large platform and hoping it works. We're building targeted solutions for specific high-value workflows and measuring results as we go. The early indicators are very encouraging."*

---

**Q: "Are you worried about data privacy or regulatory risk with AI in healthcare?"**

**BARRY PORT:**
*"It was actually one of the first things we addressed. Our architecture is HIPAA-compliant from the ground up. We have signed Business Associate Agreements with our cloud providers. Patient data never leaves the compliant environment. Every AI-generated recommendation includes human oversight before any clinical or billing action is taken. We've also engaged our legal and compliance teams from day one — this isn't a technology project running ahead of governance. It's a fully integrated initiative."*

---

**Q: "How does this compare to what other post-acute providers are doing with AI?"**

**CHAD KEETCH (CIO):**
*"I think the honest answer is that AI adoption in skilled nursing is still very early — industry data suggests less than 5% of nursing homes are meaningfully using AI today. We see that as an enormous opportunity. Our scale, our data, and our clinical track record give us advantages that we believe are very difficult to replicate. We're not waiting for our software vendors to add AI features to their existing platforms. We're building capabilities that are specifically designed for how Ensign operates."*

---

**Q: "Is this related to staffing? Are you looking to reduce headcount?"**

**SPENCER BURTON (COO):**
*"Absolutely not. This is about doing more with the incredible team we already have. When you can reduce the time a nurse spends on documentation by an hour a day, that's an hour they spend with patients. When you can give a facility CEO real-time insight into their census trajectory and payer mix, they make better decisions faster. Our turnover is already declining. Our DON retention is at all-time highs. AI makes those jobs better, not obsolete. In an industry facing a structural workforce shortage, giving our caregivers better tools is one of the smartest investments we can make."*

## SECTION 4: WHAT ENSIGN SHOULD ABSOLUTELY NOT SAY

### The Don'ts — PR Red Lines

| Never Say | Why | Say Instead |
|-----------|-----|-------------|
| "We're using ChatGPT / Claude / GPT-4" | Names the commodity. Removes perception of proprietary advantage. | "We've built proprietary AI capabilities on enterprise-grade infrastructure" |
| "We're replacing [any job function]" | Immediate media/political backlash. Healthcare workforce is a hot-button issue. | "We're giving our caregivers tools to spend more time with patients" |
| "This will save us $X million" | Invites scrutiny. If you miss the number, it's a negative story. Competitors can benchmark. | "We expect net-positive ROI within the first year" |
| "We hired an AI team of 6 people" | Sounds small. Invites comparison to tech companies with thousands. | "We've assembled a dedicated team" |
| "We're automating workflows" | Sounds like cost-cutting. Sounds like replacing people. | "We're deploying intelligent tools that support clinical decision-making" |
| "Our vendors were too slow" | Burns bridges. Makes current tech relationships adversarial. | "We chose to build capabilities specifically designed for post-acute care" |
| "We're using AWS Bedrock / Azure OpenAI" | Reveals the architecture. Competitors can copy. | "Enterprise-grade, HIPAA-compliant infrastructure" |
| "Andrew is leading this" | Makes it a single-person story. Creates key-person risk narrative. | Simply don't name individuals outside the C-suite |
| Specific facility names or pilot sites | Competitors could target those markets. Media could visit. | "Several operations across our portfolio" |
| Timelines or roadmaps | Creates accountability for things that may shift. | "We look forward to sharing more as the year unfolds" |

### The Do's — Language That Works

| Always Say | Why |
|------------|-----|
| "Proprietary" | Creates perception of moat and defensibility |
| "Purpose-built for post-acute care" | Differentiates from generic AI tools |
| "Our data" / "Our dataset" | Signals the real competitive advantage |
| "Human-in-the-loop" / "Human oversight" | Addresses the #1 concern regulators, media, and patients have |
| "HIPAA-compliant" | Table stakes but must be said explicitly |
| "Amplify" / "Augment" / "Support" | Never "replace" or "automate" in external comms |
| "Our local leaders" / "Our caregivers" | Reinforces the Ensign culture story that investors love |
| "Early results are encouraging" | Signals progress without committing to numbers |
| "Disciplined investment" | Matches Ensign's existing financial narrative |
| "Mission to dignify post-acute care" | Ties AI directly to the company's stated purpose |

## SECTION 7: MEDIA PREP — IF A REPORTER CALLS

### Scenario: Healthcare trade publication wants a story

**Approved quote (Barry Port):**
*"We've always believed that great clinical outcomes drive everything — census, payer mix, reputation, growth. AI gives our local leaders another tool to deliver those outcomes, and we're excited about what we're building. But this is about our mission, not about technology for technology's sake."*

**If asked about job losses:**
*"We're in an industry with a structural workforce shortage. Our challenge isn't having too many people — it's making sure the people we have can focus on what matters most, which is patient care. AI helps us do that."*

**If asked about cost:**
*"We're investing at a level that's consistent with our disciplined approach to capital allocation, and we expect attractive returns."*

**If asked about specific technology:**
*"We've built this on enterprise-grade, HIPAA-compliant infrastructure. Beyond that, we consider our technology architecture to be proprietary and competitively sensitive."*

**If asked about risks:**
*"We've addressed compliance, data security, and clinical governance from day one. Every AI-generated insight includes human review before any action is taken. This is healthcare — we take that responsibility very seriously."*

### Scenario: A competitor asks a mutual contact what Ensign is doing

**The story should be:** *"Ensign has some kind of AI initiative but nobody really knows the details. They're being very quiet about it."*

**That mystery IS the strategy.** When competitors can't figure out what you're doing, they can't copy it. When investors hear whispers of something big but don't have details, imagination fills the gap — usually favorably.

---

## BOTTOM LINE

The best PR strategy for Ensign's AI initiative is the same as the best military strategy: **be everywhere, be invisible, and let the results announce themselves.**

Say enough to signal conviction to investors. Say nothing that helps competitors. Lead with mission and patients. Frame AI as an amplifier of Ensign's existing culture, not a replacement for it. And never, ever name the tools, the team, or the timeline.

When the Q2 or Q3 numbers come in and the organic growth metrics accelerate beyond what the Street expected, Barry can simply say: *"Our investments in technology and our local leaders' ability to leverage those tools are contributing to the results you're seeing."*

That's it. That's the whole PR strategy. Let the numbers talk.
